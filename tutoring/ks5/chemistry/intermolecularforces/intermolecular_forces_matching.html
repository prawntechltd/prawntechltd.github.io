Answer key
1 → A London dispersion forces are the only intermolecular force present in all substances
2 → B Dipole–dipole interactions occur only between polar molecules
3 → C Hydrogen bonding is the strongest common intermolecular force
4 → D An instantaneous dipole forms when electrons temporarily shift to one side
5 → E An induced dipole is caused by a neighbouring temporary dipole
6 → F A permanent dipole results from unequal electron sharing and persists
7 → G δ+ marks the hydrogen (or less electronegative) end of a polar bond
8 → H δ− marks the more electronegative end of a polar bond
9 → I Electronegativity is the tendency of an atom to attract bonding electrons
10 → J A lone pair is the electron pair targeted in hydrogen bonding
11 → K A polar covalent bond involves unequal electron sharing
12 → L N, O, and F are the three elements that enable hydrogen bonding
